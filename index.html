News (Month/Day/Year)v1.3.3

(09/06/25) Small Fixes & Changes (v1.3.3)

A bug has been fixed where users with capital letters in their name would be given 2 seperate accounts with split data. Bands added has been reverted back to points, and now adding releases gives points too. Recently someone has added 70 bands with no info on them, so please contribute to those instead of making new ones.
Expect the following features next: Monthly bands/releases added stat, multiple versions of a release, multiple labels on a single release, improved discogs import, and bandcamp import (?, if possible).

(09/03/25) Discogs Import + More (v1.3.2)

You can now import data from Discogs when editing releases. All it takes it a link and the data will automatically be added. Band pages have also been redesigned a bit to be more space efficient and user friendly. Adding band pictures and logos has been added back too.

(09/01/25) Social Update (v1.3.0)

Users have been revamped to feature profile pictures, favorites, roles, collections, and reviews. Your profile picture can be edited on your user-specific page. The profile picture will display on your stories and reviews. You favorites can be edited in the same way. They include an optional band, label, and zine that will display on your user page. Roles have always existed on the site, but now you can see them. The roles currently available are "Top 3 Contributor", "Trusted User", and "Moderator". Collections allow you to save releases to your user page. Whether you want to share albums you like, flex records you own, or even just save releases to listen to later, collections are the way to go. Lastly, you can now add reviews to releases. Each review will feature your rating out of 10, and a body of text for you to go more in depth.

As of later today 1.3.1 has released which adds achievements, individual pages for band members, and 3 images at the bottom of this page that can be edited by the top 3 contributors.

What Is Punk Archives?

Punk Archives is basically just the punk equivalent of metal-archives. Anyone can (and is encouraged to) submit info on any punk-related bands.

Important Features To Know:

•Refresh with ctrl+shift+r if site updates aren't showing.
•Hover your mouse over a release's name to see the image.
•In All Bands, you can search by artist name, record label, genre, location, and release name.
•Bands marked with a red flag are either incomplete or inaccurate.
